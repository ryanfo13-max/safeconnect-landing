Common Questions We Hear From Fleet Operators

Common Questions We Hear From Fleet Operators

A facility's power requirements depend on the number of refrigerated trailers that will be connected simultaneously, their dwell times, and the available electrical capacity needed to support peak demand.
Most existing facilities can be upgraded to support eTRU through electrical service enhancements, power distribution improvements, and infrastructure modifications tailored to their operational needs.
ROI is typically driven by a combination of reduced diesel consumption, lower maintenance costs, extended equipment life, and minimizing operational disruptions through proper infrastructure planning.
Implementation timelines vary based on existing electrical infrastructure and utility requirements, but projects typically move fastest when power availability, capacity planning, and facility design are addressed early in the process.
The most common infrastructure mistakes are underestimating power requirements, delaying utility coordination, and designing electrical systems without accounting for real-world trailer flow and yard operations.
