Common Questions We Hear From Fleet Operators

Power requirements vary based on fleet size, trailer mix, and simultaneous plug-in targets. Most facilities need between 200–600 amps of dedicated capacity, but site surveys are essential before any infrastructure investment. This guide walks through the variables that drive that number.
Yes — in most cases, existing facilities can be upgraded without major structural changes. The key variables are available panel capacity, yard layout, and utility feed sizing. This guide covers common upgrade scenarios and what to expect during the assessment process.
Fuel savings, idle reduction, and equipment longevity are the biggest ROI drivers. The largest variable is how much of your fleet is parked at the facility overnight. Facilities with high overnight dwell times typically see payback in 2–4 years depending on fleet size and utility rates.
A typical rollout from assessment to fully operational runs 4–9 months. Utility interconnection timelines are the most common source of delays, followed by permitting. This guide includes a sample project timeline and tips for reducing lead time on the longest-lead items.
The most common mistakes are undersizing the electrical service, failing to account for future fleet growth, and skipping a proper yard flow analysis before placing pedestals. This guide documents the top 7 infrastructure planning errors and how to avoid them.
